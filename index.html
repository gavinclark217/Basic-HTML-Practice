Steps to begin DSO imaging session

  1. Find a target to capture
  2. Set your camera settings to where you want them to be
    • What will your exposure length be? ISO? Aperture? How long are you planning to be out?
    • It is very important that you capture in RAW format, not jpg!
  3. Set camera up on equatorial mount outside
  4. Point tracker North towards Polaris (North Star) if you're in the Northern hemisphere to polar align. If you're in the Southern hemisphere, point your tracker towards Octans (this is where the South Celestial Pole is)
    • Polar alignment can take a while, don't rush it or else your stars will be trailed!
    • Not all mounts require absolutely perfect polar alignment, things like guidescopes used for autoguiding can help get more accurate tracking without having your polar alignment perfect.
  5. Without messing up your alignment, point your camera towards your target
